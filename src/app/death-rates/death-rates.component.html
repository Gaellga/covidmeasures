The figures above can help you assess if COVID-19 is a threat to your life or your beloved ones. There is no simple answer and it will depend on your perception of death in your day-to-day. Ask yourself the following questions: Before COVID-19, were you regularly scared of dying or losing a beloved one? Was it something you were thinking about often?

If the answer is No. Then you shouldn't probably change your perception because of the coronavirus. Even a 50% increase in something you are not scared of, should not make you change from "I don't think about it" to "I am scared now every day". Remember that chances of dying tomorrow or next month are usually extremely low and that we live on average 26,718 days.

If the answer is Yes. Depending on your age and where you live, you should be slightly more scared because of the coronavirus. But on the bright side, you should look at all the other causes of death and evaluate the ones that have dismissed due to COVID-19 measures. Recent statistics on death rates published by the French government show that the death rate in France for March 2020 was lower or equivalent than the years prior despite COVID-19[1]."

Historical points of reference to consider in your thinking:

  • if you were an allies soldier debarking on Normandy beaches on D-Day, your chances of dying that day would be multiplied by 750 times compared to a normal day[2];
  • for any living human, chances of dying from Spanish flu in 1918 were 2,744 times higher than from COVID-19 between January and March 2020[3].
