School Closure

In their fight against COVID-19, governments around the world have decided to close schools, universities, daycare, and other educational institutions as part of the largest shutdown ever recorded. They intend to protect children from exposure to the virus as well as reducing its spread among the population. We shouldnâ€™t overlook that these measures, as legitimate as they are from the epidemic mitigation standpoint, are causing an unprecedented toll on children's education and will have long-term consequences. The CovidMeasures Community is compiling information to help citizens better assess the pros and cons of these measures as the crisis evolves.

According to our count, 1.8 Billion children corresponding that more than two-thirds of the children population on the planet have been impacted or are impacted by measures of the school closure. These measures have largely been introduced on every continent. So far children have missed on average 1 month of school with a lack of clarity on the end of the measures. Recent announcements in some countries suggest that it may last until the summer.
